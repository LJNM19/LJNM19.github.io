Ancient History

Born in blood, the Ghost of Sparta rose from mortal rage to defy the divine. Betrayed by Olympus, Kratos carved his legend across realms slaying gods, titans, and monsters alike. From the ashes of vengeance to the frostbitten wilds of new worlds, his saga echoes through time.

Mount Olympus

Crowned in clouds and forged by gods, Mount Olympus stands as the immortal throne of divine power where storms are born, destinies are written, and immortals wage eternal wars in the sky. Its fall marked the end of an era, brought by Kratos's unyielding vengeance.

The Saga of Kratos

The Greek Era A decorated Spartan general, Kratos was tricked by Ares into murdering his own wife and child. This act bound him to the Blades of Chaos and set him on a relentless path of vengeance. He hunted down and killed Ares to become the new God of War, but his torment continued. Betrayed by Zeus and the Olympians, Kratos unleashed the Titans and systematically destroyed the entire Greek pantheon, culminating in the flooding of the world.
The Norse Era Surviving his war with Olympus, a much older Kratos found his way to the lands of Norse mythology. He took a new wife, Faye, and raised a son, Atreus. After Faye's death, their journey to scatter her ashes from the highest peak in all the realms thrust them into the complex and brutal world of the Aesir and Vanir gods. Here, Kratos battles his inner rage, striving to break the cycle of patricide and be a true father.
